How is CGPA calculated at SRMIST?

For students wondering exactly how cgpa is calculated, it relies on a weighted average of your grade points and course credits. For each subject you take, the specific grade points you earn (from O equating to 10 points down to F equating to 0 points) are multiplied by the credits assigned to that specific course by the university. The total sum of these weighted points across all your registered subjects is then divided by the total number of credits you attempted in that time period. This standard mathematical formula is exactly how is cgpa calculated by our automated engine, ensuring absolute precision for your academic records.

How to calculate percentage from CGPA?

Many university students actively search for a cgpa to percentage calculator when submitting placement applications, writing resumes, or applying for higher education. If you want to know how to calculate percentage from cgpa, the official SRM University framework makes the conversion straightforward. You simply multiply your final Cumulative Grade Point Average by 10. For instance, an 8.5 CGPA becomes exactly an 85.0 percentage. Our tool has this conversion built directly into the sticky interface at the bottom. Conversely, if you need a quick percentage to cgpa calculator in your head, you simply divide your total percentage by 10.

How to calculate CGPA from SGPA?

Another common query during result season is how to calculate cgpa from sgpa across multiple semesters. It is important to know that your CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) is not merely a simple mathematical average of your past SGPAs (Semester Grade Point Averages). Instead, you must calculate the total weighted grade points earned across all completed semesters and divide that exact number by the total cumulative credits attempted across those same semesters.
